About

I am AnjaniK. Maurya, a researcher with a passion for applied engineering physics and material science. I studied Engineering Phsycis at the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, India, and the Technical University of Munich, Germany. After that, I earned my PhD in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Bern, Switzerland. During my career, I conducted research at various institutions, including the Center for X-ray Analytics and the laboratory for Biomimetic Membranes and Textiles at Empa. Currently, I am a postdoctoral research fellow at Stanford University, where I use advanced X-ray scattering and spectroscopy techniques to investigate polymeric materials for biomedical, plastic recycling, and energy storage applications. In my free time, I enjoy reading, coding, playing badminton, and exploring new technologies. .
