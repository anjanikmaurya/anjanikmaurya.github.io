About

Anjani K. Maurya is a researcher and engineer with a wealth of experience in the fields of materials science and biomedical engineering. He earned his Bachelor of Technology in Engineering Physics from the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, India, and his Master's degree in Materials Science through the prestigious Erasmus Mundus program, studying at the University of Rennes 1 in France and the Technical University of Munich in Germany.


Throughout his career, Anjani has worked with several leading research institutions, including the Swiss Federal Laboratories for Materials Science and Technology (Empa) and the University of Bern in Switzerland. At Empa, he was involved in research projects at the Center for X-ray Analytics and the Laboratory for Biomimetic Membranes and Textiles, while at the University of Bern, he earned his Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering, further developing his expertise in materials science and its applications in the biomedical field.


Currently, Anjani is a Postdoctoral Research Scholar at Stanford University and the SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory. His ongoing projects include investigating plastic recycling through chemical and biological methods to gain a deeper understanding of plastic deconstruction. He is also using X-ray-based multiscale and multimodal techniques to investigate various soft materials to better understand their structure-properties relationships.


Anjani is driven by his passion for innovation and dedication to making a meaningful impact through his work in society.
