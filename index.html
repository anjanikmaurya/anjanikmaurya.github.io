About

I am a research scholar with a wealth of experience in the fields of materials science and biomedical engineering. I earned my Bachelor of Technology in Engineering Physics from the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati, India, and my Master's degree in Materials Science through the Erasmus Mundus Master in materials science exploring large scale facilities (MaMaSELF) program, studying at the University of Rennes 1 in France and the Technical University of Munich in Germany.


Throughout my career, I have had the opportunity to work with several leading research institutions, including the Swiss Federal Laboratories for Materials Science and Technology (Empa) and the University of Bern in Switzerland. During my time at Empa, I was involved in research projects at the Center for X-ray Analytics and the Laboratory for Biomimetic Membranes and Textiles. Later, at the University of Bern, I earned my Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering, which further developed my expertise in materials science and its applications in the biomedical field.


Currently, I am a Postdoctoral Research Scholar at Stanford University and the SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory. My ongoing projects include investigating plastic recycling through chemical and biological methods to gain a deeper understanding of plastic deconstruction. Additionally, I am using X-ray-based multiscale and multimodal techniques to investigate various soft materials to better understand their structure-properties relationships.


I am driven by my passion for innovation and my dedication to making a meaningful impact through my work in society.
